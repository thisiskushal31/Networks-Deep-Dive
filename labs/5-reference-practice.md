## Computer network cheat sheet

A **cheat sheet** is a **quick reference** for **key** concepts: **OSI/TCP/IP** layers, **common ports** (80, 443, 22, 53, 67/68), **subnet** math (e.g. /24 = 256 addresses), **TCP flags**, **DHCP** DORA, **DNS** record types. **GeeksforGeeks** and other curricula publish **Computer Network – Cheat Sheet** and **Last Minute Notes**; use them for **revision** and **exam** prep. Source: [GeeksforGeeks – Computer Network Cheat Sheet](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/computer-networks/computer-network-cheat-sheet/) (see LINKS.md).

---

## Last minute notes

**Last minute notes** are **condensed** revision material: **definitions** (e.g. TCP vs UDP, CIDR, NAT), **formulas** (e.g. number of hosts in a subnet), **protocol** summaries (e.g. DNS, DHCP, HTTP). Useful before **exams** or **interviews**. Source: [GeeksforGeeks – Last Minute Notes for Computer Networks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/computer-networks/last-minute-notes-computer-network/) (see LINKS.md).

## Interview questions

**Networking** interviews often cover: **OSI/TCP/IP** layers, **TCP** (handshake, flow/congestion control, states), **UDP** vs **TCP**, **IP** (subnetting, public/private, NAT), **DNS**, **DHCP**, **HTTP/HTTPS**, **load balancing**, **firewalls**, **troubleshooting** (e.g. “user cannot reach server”). **Curated lists:** **Top 50 Plus Networking Interview Questions**, **Top 50 TCP/IP Interview Questions**, **Network Fundamentals Interview Questions** (GeeksforGeeks and similar). Source: LINKS.md (GFG interview Q&A).

---

## Practice and CTF

**Practice:** **Packet Tracer** and **GNS3** labs; **tcpdump**/ **Wireshark** on your own traffic; **mini-projects** (e.g. DHCP server, DNS resolver). **CTF and training:** **Hack The Box**, **TryHackMe**, **OverTheWire** (network and security challenges); **PicoCTF** and **edu-resources** (CSIRT-MU) for **challenges** and **training grounds**. Use **isolated** lab environments. Source: CSIRT-MU edu-resources, Hamed233 roadmap, LINKS.md.
